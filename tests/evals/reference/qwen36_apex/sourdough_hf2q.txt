   *Introduction:* Briefly explain what sourdough is, why it takes time, and set expectations (patience, temperature, consistency).

   *Phase 1: Starter Preparation*
   - Need an active, fed starter (100% hydration)
   - Feed schedule: 12-24 hrs before baking
   - Readiness test: float test, bubbles, rise/fall pattern
   - Amount needed: ~100g for a standard loaf

   *Phase 2: Mixing & Autolyse*
   - Ingredients: Bread flour, water, salt, active starter
   - Autolyse: Mix flour + water, rest 30-60 mins (develops gluten, improves extensibility)
   - Add starter + salt, mix until combined
   - Note: Salt inhibits yeast initially, so add after autolyse

   *Phase 3: Bulk Fermentation & Folds*
   - Bulk fermentation: 4-8 hours depending on temp
   - Stretch & folds: 3-4 sets, 30-45 mins apart
   - Purpose: strengthen gluten, distribute yeast, develop structure
   - Signs of completion: dough doubles, jiggly, web-like structure, slight rise

   *Phase 4: Shaping & Proofing*
   - Pre-shape: gentle round, rest 20-30 mins (bench rest)
   - Final shape: boule or batard, tension is key
   - Proofing: banneton, fridge retard (optional but recommended) 8-16 hrs
   - Temperature matters: 65-75°F ideal

   *Phase 5: Baking*
   - Preheat: Dutch oven or combo cooker, 500°F (260°C)
   - Score: sharp lame, 1/4" deep, confident cuts
   - Bake: covered 20 mins, uncovered 20-25 mins, total 40-45 mins
   - Internal temp: 205-210°F (96-99°C)
   - Crust color: deep golden brown

   *Phase 6: Cooling & Storage*
   - Cool completely on rack (2-4 hours) to finish crumb setting
   - Store: paper bag, airtight container, or freeze slices
   - Avoid cutting warm (gummy crumb)

   *Troubleshooting:*
   - Dense/heavy: underproofed, weak starter, low temp
   - Gummy: underbaked, cut too early, high hydration without technique
   - Flat/spread: overproofed, weak gluten, insufficient folds
   - Sourness: longer fermentation, cooler temps, higher starter ratio

   *Pro Tips:*
   - Hydration adjustments
   - Temperature control
   - Scaling up/down
   - Recipe flexibility

   *Safety/Hygiene:*
   - Clean tools, avoid metal contamination if possible, but note that modern stainless steel is fine
   - Food safety: sourdough's acidity makes it safe, but watch for mold
